I've broken a taboo and created a Star Wars (and its even better Spaceballs parody) and a Star Trek TNG / DS9 (as well as 2020s real online / offline life) crossover: Queen Padmé Tales. The story so far: slashdot.org's darling Israeli-American filmmaker Natalie Portman has realised that Hollywood's disapproval of crossovers / parodies / etc and its insistence of accepting only screenplays in its draconian, finnicky, and boring format has deprived it of almost all honest hobbyist screenplay writers and made most big name "acting-for-fun" big name actors "unemployed".

So she licensed a few franchises (not that a big studio will dare sue her because its share value will plumet) and decided to direct or codirect the 1992-born YouTube pop singer and budding actress Tiffany Alvord as Portman's iconic Queen Padmé Amidala, due to Alvord having better Internet-savviness.

Queen Padmé is 10 years into her reign, and needs to handle a range of issues, such as her first assassination attempt.
